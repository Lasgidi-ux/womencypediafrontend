Our Approach

Research & Editorial Method

How we ensure every story meets the highest standards of historical accuracy, cultural sensitivity, and scholarly rigor.

search
Step 1

Rigorous Research

We source primary documents, verified archives, oral histories, and peer-reviewed academic journals to build a factual foundation for every entry.

history_edu
Step 2

Interpretive Analysis

Historians and cultural experts contextualize facts, interpreting the significance of each woman's life within her specific era, culture, and social context.

public
Step 3

Scholarly Publication

Final entries are peer-reviewed by our editorial board and published as open-access resources for educators, students, researchers, and the public.

Editorial Standards

Our commitment to excellence in every biography we publish.

fact_check

Fact Verification

Every biographical claim is verified against multiple sources. We prioritize primary documents and academic scholarship over secondary sources.

diversity_3

Cultural Sensitivity

We consult with cultural experts and community representatives to ensure respectful, accurate portrayals that honor each woman's heritage.

balance

Balanced Perspective

We present women as complex individuals, acknowledging both achievements and challenges without hagiography or sensationalism.

source

Source Transparency

Every entry includes full citations and source documentation, allowing readers to verify information and pursue deeper research.

For Contributors

Guidelines for submitting stories and nominations to Womencypedia.

1

Accuracy First

Provide accurate information to the best of your knowledge. Include dates, locations, and specific details whenever possible.

2

Cite Your Sources

Include any known sources: books, articles, family records, oral histories, or archives that support the story.

3

Respect Privacy

For living individuals or recent history, ensure you have appropriate permissions and respect privacy boundaries.

4

Cultural Context

Help us understand the cultural, historical, and social context in which the woman lived and worked.
